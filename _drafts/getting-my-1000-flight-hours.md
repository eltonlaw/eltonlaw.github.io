One of [NASA's requirements to become an astronaut is:](https://www.nasa.gov/audience/forstudents/postsecondary/features/F_Astronaut_Requirements.html)

>At least three years of related professional experience obtained after degree completion OR at least 1,000 hours pilot-in-command time on jet aircraft.

My first thought was "maybe I could get that 1000 hours". It'd be neat to have that experience. I'd learn a lot of theory about how planes work while simultaneously experiencing it first hand as I flew. It would be an asset if I ever decided to apply as an astronaut candidate. Not too clear on this but it could be of use in case of specific emergencies. There would be the convenience of zipping down to another province whenever. Plus it'd just be really cool, I've always been a bit drawn in to adventure with the Ortieg Prize, Lindbergh and the Spirit of St. Louis.

Looking at the first article on Google, ["10 Ways Pilots Can Build Flight Time"](https://www.thebalancecareers.com/ways pilots can build flight time 282889), I got the impression that I would probably have to do quit my full time. Towing, flying passengers, hanging around people that were about to go flying or joining the military all involve following/flying according to somewhat of a schedule, in the sense that it wasn't an at-my-convenience kind of thing.
