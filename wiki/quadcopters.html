Brushless DC Motor

More efficient and longer lasting than brushed DC motors

There are designations such as 2204, 2212 etc., these refer to the physical dimensions of the stator. For example for 2207 the stator diameter is 22mm stator diameter and the stator height is 7mm. A larger magnetic field generates more torque for the same amount of spin so increasing stator height increases the amount of windings and increasing stator diameter increases the surface area wrapped also increasing the total windings. The tradeoff is that a large magnetic field requires more energy and will reduce the motor's speed and responsiveness.

1800 KV is perfect for a 6S battery

This isn't exactly accurate but you can think of 1900 KV as 1900 RPM per volt of input. So the bigger that number the faster it'll spin for a given voltage.

There are counterclockwise and clockwise motors and those don't have anything to do with the direction the motor spins because if you just rearrange the 3 wires connecting the ESC to the motor you can reverse the direction. The counterclockwise and clockwise descriptions are about the direction to tighten the nut holding the propellor down. If the motor spins in the opposite direction that the nut gets tightened, it will further tighten the nut.

Frame

Two common standard sizes: 20mm or 30mm spacing between holes. The flight controller and ESC usually stack on top of each other in the middle.

A larger frame is more stable. Racing drones tend to be smaller for better manueverability

Propellers

A propellor is defined in terms of diameter and pitch.

Pitch is defined as the distance a propellor will travel in one rotation. Higher pitch decreases RPM

5 inches in diameter are most common

Flight controller

Quadcopters don't fly well without precisely managing the motors with the help of sensors

Electronic Speed Controller (ESC)

Brushless motors need an external component to switch the current in the windings so that the rotor spins efficiently and in the speed and direction we want.

Batteries

Lithium Polymer batteries

Cell count: 6S means there are 6 cells wired in series where each cell has 3.7V nominal charge and 4.2V maximum charge. A 6S battery has 22.2V nominal and 25.2V max charge.

You generally want lower amperage because there's less wear on components.

Physics

Assuming center of gravity is centered on craft

Diagonal propellers spin one way and the other diagonal spins another way. This is so that the spinning motion of two propellers can be cancelled out by the exact opposite spinning motion of the other two propellors.

To hover the total lift generated by all four propellors has to equal the weight of the copter.

From hovering, to ascend you just need to increase the speed of the propellors/throttle for all four equally. To descend, you equally decrease the speed of the props/throttle

To pitch, where the copter is tilted forward or backward, you adjust the lift generated in the forward and backward propellors. To tilt forward, increase the lift of the back propellors and decrease the same amount of lift from the front propellors. To tilt backward do the opposite.

To roll, where the copter is tilted left or right, you do the same thing as pitch except for the left and right sides.

To yaw, where the copter is rotated clockwise or counter clockwise, you adjust the pairs of diagonal motors. So if you want it to spin counterclockwise. You increase the speed of the motors spinning counterclockwise and decrease the speed of the motors spinning clockwise.They're usually equal to counter the balance so as soon as you disrupt that they start spinning.
